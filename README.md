## Here is some experience sharing how I prepared for the Databricks Data Engineer Associate Certification Exam (before May 4, 2026)

**Time:** ~1 month  
**Cost:** $250 for exam registration + $200 for the lab

---

## Phase 1: Learn on Databricks Academy
Recommended courses (in order):
- Get Started with Databricks for Data Engineering
- Data Ingestion with Lakeflow Connect
- Build Data Pipelines with Lakeflow Spark Declarative Pipelines
- Deploy Workloads with Lakeflow Jobs
- Automated Deployment with Declarative Automation Bundles
- Get Started with Data Governance on Databricks

---

## Phase 2: Use the Databricks Free Edition (if you don't purchase the lab)
In the **left** navigation bar, select **"Learn"**, then you can choose to do courses or demos.

---

## Phase 3: Practice Quiz
I recommend using **CertSafari** — it's free and the explanations are rich.  
The questions won't necessarily be identical to your actual exam, but they are very close in phrasing.  
🔗 https://www.certsafari.com/databricks

I did all of them on my laptop and used my phone to start 25/50 question quizzes during my commute, so I completed around 500–700 questions in total (with some overlap).

---

## On the Exam Day (Online Proctored)
- Log in **15–30 minutes in advance** to complete all setup and configurations.
- You are **not allowed** to wear a necklace or watch.
- There are **52 multiple choice questions** in total, with approximately **4 requiring two selections**.
- You will know your result **instantly** after completing the test.
- Your score (percentage) is broken down **by chapter**.
